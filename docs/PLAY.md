# 🏖️ Kitchen Rush — How to Play

Hi! This is the player guide. It's written for grown-ups setting it up for kids, and kids who can read it themselves. There's no fail-state in this game, so you can't break anything — try stuff!

---

## Getting in

1. Open Safari (or Chrome / Edge / Firefox) on the iPad, iPhone, or any computer on the same Wi-Fi.
2. Go to the URL the grown-up gave you. Probably something like `http://192.168.1.50:5050`.
3. Type a username. Just a name — no password, no email. The game makes the account for you.
4. Tap **Continue** → you're in.

If this is your first time, a friendly **Welcome card** pops up explaining the game in three bullets. Tap *"Open the shop"* when you're ready.

## What you actually do (the verbs)

There are only six things you can do. That's the whole game.

### 1. Tap a cook to coach them

Touch any cook on the canvas (the cute cartoon chefs at the stations) OR open the **Team panel** on the right side and tap a row. A coaching modal pops up with six friendly phrases:

- ⭐ **"Nice work, keep it up!"** — Boosts mood. Cooks like hearing this.
- 🌊 **"Take your time."** — Calm reassurance. Tiny mood boost.
- 🔁 **"Try the recipe again."** — Gentle correction. Costs a bit of mood but teaches skill (XP).
- 🔥 **"Watch your station's heat."** — Technique tip. Skill XP.
- 🎫 **"Check your order ticket."** — Focus tip.
- 🧹 **"Cleanup when you can."** — Tiny mood dip, helps cleanliness.

Each phrase has a short **cooldown** (you'll see it fade for a few seconds). Use praise often, corrections sparingly.

> **What's mood?** Your cook's happiness, 0 to 100. Higher mood = better service score on reviews. Cooks start each day at 65+ and lose a tiny bit as they cook — coaching helps top them up.

### 2. Tap a table to chat with a customer

Touch any table on the canvas where someone's sitting. You'll see:

- 📷 **Who they are** (Beach Bum, Tourist Family, Foodie Critic, etc.)
- 😊 **Their mood** with a face and a bar
- 🍔 **What they ordered** and how long they've been waiting
- 💬 **A friendly question** they want to ask the owner ("Got any hot sauce? 🌶️")
- Three big answer buttons → tap one → they say thanks → modal closes

This is just for fun. No score is changed, no money lost. It's a sticker-book moment.

### 3. Hire (top bar 👤+)

Up to 4 cooks total. Each hire costs $100–$250 up front + $40/day wages.

The hire panel shows 3 randomly generated candidates with **suggested stations** based on what your team needs. Pick a station from the dropdown, tap **Hire**.

### 4. Set prices and unlock recipes (top bar 📖)

The Menu modal has two sections:

- **Your Menu** — recipes you've already unlocked. Tap a price to edit it. Charge more → more cash per order but customers tip less. Charge less → more customers, less per order.
- **Available to Unlock** — recipes you can buy in for $25–$250. Buying unlocks them forever.

You start with **Classic Burger ($8)**, **French Fries ($4)**, **Cola ($3)**.

### 5. Clean (top bar 🧹)

Cleanliness drops slowly during open hours (a couple of points per in-game hour). When it drops, review scores on the "cleanliness" dimension drop too. Tap 🧹 → +30 cleanliness. Easy.

### 6. Read reviews (top bar ⭐)

Every customer who eats here writes a review. You see:
- The customer's persona
- A 1-sentence quote in their voice (*"Solid grindz, mate. Cola was choice."*)
- Six dimension scores (taste, cleanliness, seating, service, vibe, timing)
- A friendly improvement hint

Reviews from the **last 20** orders set your overall **reputation** (0–100), which affects how many customers show up tomorrow.

## The rhythm of a day

A real-world **5 minutes = one in-game day**. Inside that day:

- **5–8 AM (quiet hours):** Nobody's around. Game runs slower. Good time for menu changes or hiring.
- **8 AM:** Doors open. First customers trickle in.
- **12–2 PM:** Lunch rush. Most customers of the day.
- **6–9 PM:** Dinner rush. Date couples and foodies.
- **10 PM–1 AM:** Quieter, with night-owl students.
- **2–5 AM:** Wind down.

At the end of each day, you'll see a **Day Summary** card with stats and your daily score. Then the new day starts at 5 AM (quiet) → 8 AM (open).

## What the canvas tells you at a glance

Every cook and every customer has a tiny **mood face** floating above their head:

- 😊 happy — mood ≥ 65 (or customer just arrived)
- 😐 neutral — mood ~35–65 (or customer's been here a while)
- 😟 unhappy — mood < 35 (or customer's been here a long time)

Customers move through four phases visually:

| Phase | What it means |
|-------|---------|
| **Walking in** | Just arrived, slipping in from the right edge |
| **Seated** | At a table, your kitchen is working on their order |
| **Eating** | Food was served! They're enjoying it |
| **Leaving** | Done — they exit to the right and a review drops |

## The Stats panel (📊)

Tap 📊 to see your whole shop at once:

- **Team** — every cook with their portrait, level, station, mood, and what they're doing *right now* ("Cooking 🍔 for Marco" or "Idle — tap to coach").
- **Tables** — every customer in the scene with their order, status, mood, and how long they've been waiting in plain words ("Just arrived" / "Settled in" / "Been here a while" / "Patient one!").

Good place to scan when reviews start dipping — you'll usually find an idle cook or a low-mood one.

## Sounds 🔊

The game has gentle sound effects:

- 🪙 **Coin sound** when an order pays out
- 🛎 **Bell** when a customer is served
- 🎉 **Cheer** when a great review (≥80 avg) lands
- 🎶 **Chime** for good reviews (≥60)
- 🌊 **Soft falling tone** for review below 40
- ✨ **Warm two-note** when you finish coaching
- 💖 **Thank-you sound** when a customer answers a question
- 🔘 Quiet pop/click for every button tap

Turn them off in **Settings → 🔊 Sound**. Saved per-device.

## Earning more cash

The fast path:

1. **Keep cooks happy** — coach with Praise/Take-your-time. Happy cooks → high service → big tips.
2. **Keep the floor clean** — tap 🧹 a few times a day. Low cleanliness drags every review.
3. **Charge fairly** — overpriced burgers get low taste scores in reviews. Default prices are a safe baseline.
4. **Unlock variety** — the more recipes on the menu, the more customers will find something they like.
5. **Hire help during rushes** — solo Alex can handle 20 orders/day. Three cooks can handle 50+.

The slow path is just letting it run. There's no fail-state.

---

## What happens if I ignore the game

Nothing bad. Customers don't walk out. Cooks don't quit. Cash doesn't go negative (it floors at $0). Cleanliness floors at 0 (reviews will be sad-flavored, but the day still ends fine and tomorrow resets a bit).

Pause whenever. Come back whenever. **No rush** is literally on the box.

## Troubleshooting (for grown-ups)

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Page won't load on iPad | Confirm Mac and iPad are on the same Wi-Fi. Try `http://localhost:5050` on the Mac first to confirm the server is up. |
| Game stuck on "Loading…" | Tap the page once — Safari blocks audio + WebSockets until first interaction. |
| No sound | Tap any button first (audio unlocks on first gesture). Or check Settings → 🔊. |
| iPad screen looks wonky | Pull-to-refresh once. Most cropping is fixed in the latest build. |
| All my cooks have mood 0 | Old save from before the mood tuning fix. Open the **Coaching** modal and tap Praise on each cook a couple of times. They bounce back next morning anyway. |
| "Session expired" | Tap **Retry** at the bottom. Login is one-tap. |

For deeper trouble: **[docs/DEPLOY.md](./DEPLOY.md)** has the LAN/firewall section.
